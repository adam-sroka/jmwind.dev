This site and my writings are influenced by the book Drawing from the right side of the brain. While drawing was never my profession, it helped me see things that often others around me often didn't see.

Granted, I'm still blind and have rose coloured glasses, but my writings and interests are squarely focused on training my perpection skills and sharing what I see with others so that we can all get better together.

Book

I co-wrote a book in 2005, it was painful but one of the most rewarding experiences of my life. At the time my kids were aged 1 and 3 and my timing was terrible. I was away from them a lot writing at night and working during the day. Now I've got more time.

But I don't want to go away, write, and then publish everything all at once hoping that it was useful. Instead I've decided to write a book in pieces. Just the salient parts as a series of threads on Twitter.

Each micro-chapter starts with a question and answer. This format comes easily from conversions that I've had over the years. It also means less preamble, we can just jump into an answer!

I'm hopeful that micro-writing will force me to be succint and to the point. Let me know if these are useful and I'll keep publishing them.

  1. Chapter 1: How do you handle/manage the stress of working in a hyper-growth company where there's always too much to do?
  2. Chapter 2: How much of your r&d spend should be focused on platform work?
  3. Chapter 3: What virus infects growing engineering orgs the most?
